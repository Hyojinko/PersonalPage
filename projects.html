Uncertainty-Aware Meta-Learning for Robust Stent Segmentation via Selective Ensemble

Asan Institute of Life Sciences / Asan Medical Center · 2025 – Present
Deep Learning Medical Image Segmentation Selective Ensemble Meta-Learning CinC 2026
Lead Researcher — Stent Registration & Segmentation Pipeline

A deep learning pipeline for robust stent and catheter segmentation in invasive coronary angiography, developed to support post-PCI quantitative coronary analysis (QCA) and stent-vessel registration across fluoroscopy and angiography modalities.

Problem. Single segmentation models trained with different loss functions produce heterogeneous predictions with distinct failure modes—disconnected vessels, spurious branches, and boundary inaccuracies—making naive ensembling unreliable for clinical deployment.

Approach.

  • Trained five baseline models (U-Net, DenseNet-121) with morphology-focused loss functions: Dice, Centerline, Hausdorff, Edge Distance, and Topology losses
  • Developed rank-based selective ensemble methods — MSEN (Morphology-based Selective Ensemble) and ESEN (Estimated DSC-based Selective Ensemble) — that weight predictions by mask quality rather than averaging uniformly
  • Built an attention-based meta-learner that incorporates inter-model disagreement as an uncertainty map to guide robust mask fusion
  • Evaluated on five-fold cross-validation with 942 internal and 358 external angiographic projections from PCI patients

Key Results. The selective ensemble improved segmentation performance by up to 5.04% over the weakest baseline model on challenging folds, with an average improvement of up to 3.68%. The framework integrates with an end-to-end stent-vessel mapping pipeline using ICP-based catheter tip alignment and auxiliary centerline mapping.

Publication: Uncertainty-Aware Meta-Learning for Robust Stent Segmentation via Selective Ensemble — Computing in Cardiology (CinC) 2026

Centerline Extraction for Vascular Masks

Asan Institute of Life Sciences / Asan Medical Center
Centerline Morphology Segmentation

Developed geometric algorithms for robust centerline extraction from vascular segmentation masks. The method segments masks by average slope, draws perpendicular normals at 3-pixel intervals, and iteratively refines centerline points to avoid edge bias—supporting accurate vessel entrance/exit detection for downstream QCA analysis.
