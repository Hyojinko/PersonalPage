About Me

Hyojin Ko is a Researcher of medical artificial intelligence at the Asan Institute of Life Sciences/Asan Medical Center. Her research interests include artificial intelligence, image analysis, deep learning, knowledge graph, and large language models. At her current position, she focuses on deep learning–based medical image analysis integrated with large language models, aiming to develop clinical decision support systems (CDSS) that support automated medical image interpretation and structured clinical report generation. Her work emphasizes end-to-end pipelines that connect image understanding with language-based reasoning to assist clinicians in real-world diagnostic workflows. During her master's research, she studied multimodal language models that jointly process images and textual data. In parallel, she researched on explainable AI (XAI)-based CDSS, incorporating knowledge graphs to enhance model interpretability, clinical reasoning, and structured knowledge representation.

Uncertainty-Aware Meta-Learning for Robust Stent Segmentation via Selective Ensemble

Asan Institute of Life Sciences / Asan Medical Center 2025 – Present
Deep Learning Segmentation Selective Ensemble Meta-Learning CinC 2026

Lead Researcher — Stent Registration & Segmentation Pipeline

Deep learning pipeline for stent and catheter segmentation in invasive coronary angiography, supporting post-PCI QCA and stent-vessel registration across fluoroscopy and angiography.

Problem

Single models with different loss functions show distinct failure modes—disconnected vessels, spurious branches, boundary errors—making naive ensembling unreliable.

Approach

  • Five U-Net / DenseNet-121 baselines with morphology-focused losses
  • MSEN & ESEN rank-based selective ensemble
  • Attention meta-learner with uncertainty maps

Results

Up to 5.04% improvement over weakest baseline (avg. 3.68%). Evaluated on 942 internal + 358 external PCI projections with five-fold CV.

Publication: Uncertainty-Aware Meta-Learning for Robust Stent Segmentation via Selective Ensemble — Computing in Cardiology (CinC) 2026
